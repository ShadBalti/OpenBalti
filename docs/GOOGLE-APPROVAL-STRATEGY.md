## What Makes Content "High Quality" for Google

### Google's Criteria (What They Look For)

1. **Originality**
   - Original research or analysis
   - Unique perspective or insights
   - Firsthand experience described
   - Original data or statistics

2. **Depth**
   - Comprehensive coverage
   - Sufficient detail
   - Answers all related questions
   - In-depth examples

3. **Expertise**
   - Author credentials shown
   - Subject matter knowledge evident
   - Experience demonstrated
   - Authoritative voice

4. **Trustworthiness**
   - Factual accuracy
   - Sources cited
   - No misleading claims
   - Transparent about limitations

5. **User Focus**
   - Solves actual user problems
   - Clear, helpful writing
   - Well-organized
   - Easy to understand

### Red Flags (What They Avoid)

- ‚ùå Thin content (<300 words with little substance)
- ‚ùå Keyword stuffing or manipulation
- ‚ùå Duplicate content
- ‚ùå No author information on expert content
- ‚ùå Misleading titles or descriptions
- ‚ùå Auto-generated or AI content without disclosure
- ‚ùå Unsourced or unverified claims
- ‚ùå Outdated information presented as current
